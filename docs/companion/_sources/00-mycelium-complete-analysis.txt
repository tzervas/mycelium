## What a Functional Value-Semantic Programming Language Is


A functional value-semantic language is one where the fundamental unit of computation is the *value* — an immutable, self-contained piece of data whose identity is determined by what it *is*, not where it lives — and where programs are compositions of functions that transform values into new values rather than mutating state in place. Because nothing is ever modified after construction, there is no aliased mutable state: two references to "the same" value are indistinguishable from two copies of it, which structurally eliminates entire defect classes (use-after-free, data races, iterator invalidation, action-at-a-distance) rather than policing them with a borrow checker or a garbage collector's runtime discipline. Assignment and parameter passing conceptually copy (implementations optimize this away via moves, sharing, or copy-on-write, since immutability makes sharing observationally invisible), equality is structural rather than referential, and evaluation can be reasoned about equationally — substituting equals for equals — which is what makes such languages unusually amenable to formal verification, deterministic parallelism, and content-addressed identity.

### Why this framing matters for Mycelium

Mycelium takes each clause of that definition and makes it load-bearing rather than incidental. Immutability plus structurally-guaranteed acyclicity license the entire three-layer memory architecture (affine ownership, garbage-free reference counting, concurrent region reclamation — see the memory-model document). Structural identity becomes literal content addressing: a definition's identity is a BLAKE3 hash of its normalized structure, types, and contracts. And equational reasoning becomes the substrate for the certification system — the guarantee lattice, swap certificates, and discharged proof obligations all rest on the fact that a value's meaning is exhausted by its structure. The language's distinctive features are not bolted onto value semantics; they are what value semantics makes cheap.

## Mycelium: What It Is and How It's Built


### The thesis

The thesis, stated in `docs/guide/why-and-design.md` and enforced everywhere in the code, is that modern computing keeps four representation families in walled gardens — bits, balanced ternary, dense embeddings, and VSA hypervectors — and the place correctness quietly dies is the *conversion* between them. Conversions are implicit, lossy in undocumented ways, and impossible to audit. Mycelium makes the representation swap the explicit, first-class, certifiable operation of the language, and makes every approximation disclosed, bounded, and tagged with how trustworthy that bound is.

Three non-negotiables shape every decision: no hidden or opaque behavior in core semantics; dual intelligibility (human-readable *and* useful for AI agents); and formal reasoning-amenability — "no black boxes" realized as mechanically-checkable invariants, not a slogan. The original "certified-everything" premise was explicitly repositioned by ADR-032 (Enacted 2026-06-24): the north star is now a fast, memory-safe, ergonomic multi-paradigm language, with certification and transparency as optional, tunable capabilities (`fast` by default, `certified` on request) rather than a tax on every line. The honesty rule was reframed as the transparency-and-auditability rule, mechanism unchanged.

### Scale

As checked out: roughly 300K lines of Rust across 56 workspace crates plus `xtask`, about 22K lines of Mycelium (`.myc`) in `lib/std`, `lib/compiler`, and `examples/`, a 450-line normative EBNF with a 57-program accept/reject conformance corpus, and a governance corpus whose own verified digest (2026-07-09) counts 33 in-tree ADRs, 41 RFCs, and 97 design notes — with ADR numbering reaching 045, RFC numbering 0041, and DN numbering 140. Discharged proof artifacts live in `proofs/`: a Z3 SMT obligation for binary↔ternary round-trip injectivity (returns `unsat`, i.e., the injectivity property holds), a Liquid Haskell bundle, and Lean/Haskell/SMT examples for VSA multi-hop bounds.

### Architecture: a layered tower with a tiny trusted base

**L0 Core IR** (`mycelium-core`, compiled under `#![forbid(unsafe_code)]`) is a deliberately small node grammar — `Const`, `Var`, `Let`, `Op`, `Swap`, plus the r3 additions `Construct` and `Match` and the r4 additions `Lam`, `App`, `Fix` — where `Swap` is *the only node that can change a value's representation* and is required by construction to carry a `PolicyRef` (the field is mandatory, so the well-formedness rules WF1/WF2 cannot be violated by any code path). RFC-0036 later ratified the single-frozen-kernel position: nine of ten nodes judged irreducibly primitive, with `FixGroup` derivability the one flagged open question.

**`mycelium-interp`** is the reference interpreter: a small-step, call-by-value substitution semantics documented as inference rules in the crate root. It *is* the meaning of the language — the MLIR→LLVM AOT backend (`mycelium-mlir`) is differential-tested against it, never the other way around. The interpreter threads metadata honestly: an operation's result guarantee is the lattice meet of its inputs and the operation's intrinsic strength, and provenance is recorded as `Derived{op, inputs}` over content hashes. A free variable is stuck with an explicit `EvalError::FreeVariable`, never a silent default.

**L1 kernel calculus** (`mycelium-l1`) adds the type discipline: a bidirectional checker, generics and traits with dictionary-free static resolution, higher-order functions via Reynolds static defunctionalization (RFC-0024 — closures become a tag-sum struct plus a generated `apply` dispatcher, so the kernel never needs heap closures), operator syntax as pure frontend sugar desugaring to word-function calls, totality and usefulness analyses, and the elaborator that lowers everything to closed L0 terms. **L2/L3** are the surface term language and the concrete grammar (RFC-0020/RFC-0030/RFC-0037), where guarantee tags appear directly in signatures: `fn narrow(x: Dense{768, F32}) => Dense{768, BF16} @ Empirical`.

### The crate organization and why it is the way it is

The 56-crate split follows a strict dependency-strata discipline enforced by `xtask` against `deps-strata.toml` — dependency cycles between strata are refused mechanically, and at least one crate (`mycelium-vsa-decode`) exists specifically because hosting its function in `mycelium-vsa` would have closed a forbidden interp↔vsa↔select cycle. The organizing principles:

The **kernel stays small and auditable** (principle KC-3). `mycelium-core` carries only the value model, the guarantee lattice, content addressing, and the node grammar. The VSA algebra (`mycelium-vsa` — six models: MAP-I, MAP-B, BSC, FHRR, HRR, SBC, plus resonator factorization and cleanup memories) is a dependency-gated submodule: the kernel type-checks hypervector *mentions* without pulling in the algebra, so VSA is opt-in without being second-class.

**Optimization is untrusted by construction.** `mycelium-mir-passes` (the reference-count elision pass) lives outside the trusted base, so a bug there is a missed optimization, never unsoundness — the runtime reference-count probe is always the sound fallback.

**The standard library is Rust-first, mirrored in Mycelium.** Each `mycelium-std-*` crate has a corresponding `lib/std/*.myc` port produced under RFC-0031's discipline: everything expressible is ported and differentially witnessed against the live Rust oracle, and everything at the kernel boundary is FLAGGED explicitly rather than faked — "a mostly-FLAGged result is the honest outcome for a re-export facade."

**The toolchain is real**: CLI (`myc check`/`myc run`), LSP, formatter (`mycfmt`), linter, doc generator (a themed docsite rendering the whole corpus plus rustdoc), build/project system (`mycelium-proj` manifests), security scanning, a fuzzing setup, mutation testing output, and a Rust→Mycelium transpiler (`mycelium-transpile`) that is now the engine of the self-hosting program.

### Self-hosting direction

ADR-042/043 (Accepted 2026-07-07) commit the project to full Mycelium self-hosting: freeze the Rust base and progressively rewrite everything — kernel, toolchain, and codegen backend included — to Mycelium ("zero foreign first-party languages"), retiring each Rust component only once proven by a dual witness (the Rust differential plus the native `myc` toolchain) and archiving it to a protected legacy branch, never deleting. The `lib/compiler/*.myc` files (lexer, parser, AST, semantic core, totality) are the visible start of that program. Notably, the `mycelium-tero` HTTP context/memory service — the one component that pulled `tokio`/`axum` into the workspace (ADR-044) — was extracted verbatim into its own published repo (`tzervas/tero-rs`) during a trim pass, so the workspace no longer carries an async runtime at all.

### The governance culture

The project's most unusual property may be that the documentation practices what the language preaches. Every claim in the corpus is tagged at the strength its evidence actually supports — `Declared` for asserted goals, `Empirical` for measured results with the measurement named, `Proven` for machine-checked facts with the checker named — under the standing rule VR-5 (downgrade, don't overclaim) and G2 (never silent). Design notes routinely correct their own task framings on the merits ("no sycophancy" is a literal house rule), record ratification bases verbatim, and draw the landed-versus-`Declared` boundary explicitly. The result is a corpus where "what is actually true right now" is a resolvable query rather than an archaeology project — see the documentation-strategy document for the resolution chain.

## The Mycelium Data Model


A runtime value is `Value = Repr + Payload + Meta`, and each leg carries deliberate weight. This document walks each mechanism, why it is the way it is, and the pros and cons of each.

### Representation in the type

`Binary{width}` (sign-free per ADR-028), `Ternary{trits}` (balanced trits in {−1, 0, +1}, arbitrary-width arithmetic per ADR-029), `Dense{dim, dtype}`, and `VSA{model, dim, sparsity}` are the four closed paradigm kinds — adding a fifth requires an RFC plus ADR — while the parameter registries (`ScalarKind`, VSA model ids, `SparsityClass`) stay open. Later value forms (`Seq{T,N}`, `Bytes`, `Float`) were appended under their own decisions (RFC-0032, ADR-040) with frozen content-address tags so no existing value's identity shifted. There is no implicit conversion, no inference of representation from usage, and no silent insertion of coercions — RFC-0012's ambient-representation feature explicitly forbids both, letting a nodule declare `default paradigm Binary` once so `{8}` means `Binary{8}`, while missing cross-paradigm conversions are statically *refused* rather than synthesized. The payload variants mirror the reprs exactly (`Bits`, `Trits`, `Scalars`, `Hypervector`, `Float`, `Seq`, `Bytes`), and deserialization routes through `Value::new`, so a wire value whose payload mismatches its declared repr is rejected, never coerced.

**Pro:** a type signature tells you the physical-semantic story completely, and cross-paradigm bugs become type errors caught before execution. **Con:** annotation weight (which the ambient mitigates but does not eliminate), and a closed paradigm set that makes adding, say, posits or unums a governance event rather than a library.

### The guarantee lattice as load-bearing metadata

Every value's `Meta` carries a `GuaranteeStrength` from the lattice `Exact ⊐ Proven ⊐ Empirical ⊐ Declared`, and composition is the lattice *meet* — weakest-wins, with the semilattice laws (commutative, associative, idempotent, identity `Exact`) verified exhaustively over all 4×4×4 tuples, a complete check rather than sampling since the space is finite. The four honesty invariants are enforced *by construction* in `Meta::new`: M-I1 (`Exact ⟺ no bound`), and M-I2/I3/I4 (a bound's *basis* — a cited theorem with checked side-conditions, an empirical fit with trial counts and method named, or a bare user assertion — determines the honest strength). An inconsistent `Meta` is unrepresentable, and `Deserialize` re-runs the invariants so a malformed wire `Meta` is rejected, never trusted. Bounds themselves are typed (`ErrorBound` with ε and norm, `ProbabilityBound`, `CrosstalkBound`, `CapacityBound`), composing through ADR-010's verified-numerics kernels (affine arithmetic for error, union bound for probability), and ADR-011 makes the basis a required companion of *every* bound, not just capacity bounds. In the surface language the tag is a type-level index (language requirement LR-6): a function demanding `Exact` input is a type error to call with `Declared`.

**Pro:** "how much should I trust this number" becomes a queryable, monotone, type-visible property that no mainstream language can express, and the propagation rule (`guarantee(result) = meet(inputs…, intrinsic)`) is the *only* sanctioned derivation, so disclosure can degrade but never spuriously upgrade. **Con:** per-value metadata that must travel everywhere (space and plumbing cost); the weakest-wins rule is pessimistic by design — one `Declared` input poisons an entire pipeline's tag even if the declared bound happens to be true; and the tags are only as honest as the bases feeding them.

### Content-addressed identity

A definition's identity is `BLAKE3(normalize(structure) ‖ types-with-repr ‖ static-contract)` — the structure α-renamed to position-independent de Bruijn indices so binder names don't matter, the encoding domain-separated and length-prefixed so distinct structures can never collide by concatenation ambiguity, and every tag registry append-only so growing the language never shifts an existing address. Names, source spans, comments, and *all dynamic `Meta`* (provenance, measured sparsity, realized bounds, certification mode) are excluded from the hash — names live in a separable `Names` map, so renaming a definition does not change its identity. The acceptance criteria are explicit: two definitions differing only in representation paradigm hash *differently*; a renamed or reformatted definition hashes *identically*; identical definitions collide. Provenance is then an acyclic DAG of these hashes (`Root` or `Derived{op, inputs}`), and the `spore` — the deployable unit (ADR-013) — carries a kernel-minted content identity that tooling `germinate`s at a target, with precedents in Nix derivations, OCI digests, and Unison's by-hash code shipping.

Floats forced a real design fight here, resolved in ADR-040: NaN payload bits are canonicalized to a single positive quiet-NaN pattern at value construction, so identity never depends on platform NaN behavior (which hardware does not determine), while `+0.0`/`−0.0` stay bit-distinct in identity but IEEE-equal under derived `==` — a documented identity-versus-equality seam. Float wire forms are strings (`"1.5"`, `"-inf"`, `"NaN"`) because JSON numbers cannot carry IEEE specials and `serde_json` would serialize a non-finite `f64` as `null` — a silent loss the design refuses.

**Pro:** perfect structural deduplication, safe hot-injection of recompiled definitions (ADR-017), reproducible identity for the spore registry (ADR-037, GHCR/OCI backend), and provenance you can actually audit. **Con:** hashing normalized ASTs isn't free; the append-only tag discipline is a forever-tax on registry evolution; and the float seams (canonical NaN, signed-zero identity) are things a user must learn.

### Explicit swaps under a split verification regime

`Swap` is the only representation-changing operation, its target and policy always lexical. The verification regime is split (RFC-0002): binary↔ternary is the one genuinely bijective class — the encode/decode is pinned in `docs/spec/swaps/binary-ternary.md` down to digit semantics, chosen widths, range, and rounding (in balanced ternary, truncation *is* round-to-nearest and ties are impossible), and the injectivity lemma is discharged both by Z3 (`unsat` on `roundtrip_8x6.smt2`) and by exhaustive decision in Rust, with the lemma's own content hash pinned in `mycelium-cert`. Everything touching Dense or VSA is bounded-or-probabilistic, and the emitted certificate says which class it is. Lossy swaps can attach a *reconstruction manifest* (RFC-0003 §6) that inspectably distinguishes **indexed retrieval** (codebook + similarity + threshold — returns a stored atom; a bounded-lossy lookup, and the type system won't let you pretend it's more) from **true compositional reconstruction** (structural recipe of roles + algebraic inverse operations — able to recover *novel* combinations never stored, VSA's defining capability over a hash table). Resonator-based decoding is capped at `Empirical` by construction (invariant FR-C2) because probabilistic factorization is all it can honestly claim.

**Pro:** the exact place correctness leaks in ML and quantization pipelines becomes a typed, certified, EXPLAIN-able event, with the honest proof burden matched to what is actually provable per swap class. **Con:** ceremony — every conversion is a visible act carrying a policy reference, which is the point but is friction; and certificate emission and checking have real cost, which motivated the next mechanism.

### Tunable certification

RFC-0034/ADR-032 gate the certificate machinery behind three modes — `fast` (default), `balanced`, `certified` — resolved through the same scoping mechanism as the ambient (`global`/`phylum`/`nodule` via `@certification`). The mode tag itself is never-silent: every `Meta` records which mode produced it, and the mode is excluded from the content hash so switching modes never perturbs identity (the exclusion is by construction — the hasher never reads `Meta`). Certificate *emission* happens in `balanced` and `certified`; *checking* only in `certified`; `Empirical`/`Proven` tags are reachable only where the machinery that justifies them runs. The §13 conformance suite verifies every clause mode-parametrically *both ways* — each invariant must fire in the tiers it applies to and be correctly absent in the tiers it does not, so a `certified`-only invariant holding spuriously in `fast` is caught. A separate named opt-out (`wrapping { … }`) covers arithmetic wraparound: never-silent failability (`Option`/`Result`/`SwapError`) is the default in every mode, and wraparound is an explicit per-use-site annotation recorded in `Meta`, never an ambient default.

**Pro:** you pay for auditability only where you want it, resolving the classic verification-tax death spiral, and the pay-for-what-you-use posture is the same shape as the memory model's. **Con:** a `fast`-mode value's guarantees are weaker in ways downstream consumers must account for — the generation≠consumption split exists precisely because this is easy to get wrong.

### Physical packing is a schedule, not a type

How trits are packed — unpacked, two-bit-per-trit, five-trit-per-byte, or bitnet.cpp's I2_S/TL1/TL2 schemes — is chosen at a lowering stage and *recorded* in `Meta.physical` as an inspectable `PhysicalLayout`, never encoded in the type (DN-01, RFC-0004 §5). Values of the same logical type interoperate whether packed or not; the selection engine that picks the schedule is the same reified, EXPLAIN-able policy mechanism (RFC-0005) used for swap targets and runtime placement.

**Pro:** layout experiments — and BitNet-style ternary inference kernels specifically — don't fracture the type system, and the chosen layout is auditable after the fact. **Con:** the logical/physical seam means performance-critical code cannot statically demand a layout; it can only observe what the scheduler chose.

### Algebraic data as a separate category

A `Construct` node evaluates to a `Datum` — constructor tag plus field values — which is deliberately *not* a fifth paradigm; the runtime value the interpreter yields is the sum `CoreValue = Value | Datum`, mirrored between the L0 interpreter and the L1 evaluator so the two agree on the data fragment. A datum carries a single meet-summary guarantee (the meet of its fields' guarantees, construction adding no approximation) and *no bound*, because a structural composite is not itself an approximation — the quantitative bounds live on the leaf representation values and are drillable via provenance. `Match` is flat and checked-exhaustive (WF7), with the Maranget decision tree that compiles nested surface patterns kept as an untrusted artifact above the kernel.

**Pro:** real ADTs with exhaustiveness checking while the closed-paradigm invariant stays intact; the honesty story extends to composites without inventing fake bounds. **Con:** the summary is lossy by design — you know *something* inside is `Empirical`, and finding which leaf means walking provenance.

### Defensive boundaries

`Repr` deserialization enforces `MAX_DIM = 2^30` on every declared dimension field, because the wire forms carry dimensions as `u32` and a crafted descriptor declaring a four-billion-element hypervector is a latent over-allocation denial-of-service on the deserialize path; the rejection names the offending field, value, and cap. The check is `Exact` (a total decidable predicate); the cap value itself is honestly tagged `Declared` (a policy choice, not a fact about any value) — a small but characteristic example of the tagging discipline applied to the language's own internals.

### Current frontier (from the document tail)

Three recent decisions extend this model. **RFC-0039** (Accepted 2026-06-30) gives the physical leg an AOT enforcement story: native Dense element-wise lowering with ADR-030 quantization descriptors, native VSA bind/bundle/permute across all six models honoring ADR-031's element-space/sparsity/complex-carrier commitments, and a dynamic-VSA JIT as an explicitly-selected `ExecMode` — everything recorded in `Meta.physical` and differential-checked against the reference interpreter. **DN-126** (Accepted 2026-07-12, `Declared`/unbuilt) ratifies type-resolution *strictness* as a genuinely third orthogonal axis — distinct from certification depth and from guarantee grade — with loose duck-typed checking on the interpreted path, strict typing gating compilation unconditionally, and mechanical strictification that writes down only *principal* inferred types, surfacing non-principal holes never-silently. **DN-120** formally closed ADR-003's disclosed residual on content-addressed identity versus efficient temporary-copy mutation (solved-by-design via the `rc == 1` reuse side-condition; see the memory-model document).

## The Mycelium Memory Model


### The foundation: what value semantics buys

Mycelium values are immutable and *acyclic* — immutability makes cycles unconstructible, and acyclicity is exactly Perceus's garbage-free precondition, which RFC-0027 leans on to justify precise reference counting as the reclamation mechanism without ever needing a cycle collector. The language rules make the outcome explicit (LR-9): Rust-grade safety *outcomes* without Rust's mechanism. Value semantics removes use-after-free, data races, and double-free from the language model outright; the language exposes no manual allocation or free; reclamation is automatic and deterministic; the only leak vector — an unreleased *external resource* — is closed by the affine `Substrate` kind (the LR-8 position: Rust-style ownership/borrowing is "likely not applicable" to a value substrate, with linearity reserved for external resources only); and anything that could violate this (raw FFI, foreign memory) is unreachable from safe code — it must appear in a lexically-marked `wild` block, denied by default, legal only in an `@std-sys` nodule whose enclosing function declares the `!{ffi}` effect. The stated guarantee: *in safe Mycelium a memory leak is not expressible.*

The reference interpreter deliberately does not participate in any of the cleverness below — it is substitution-based, values cloned into the tree, because it is the trusted *meaning*. Performance lives in the AOT path and the runtime memory architecture, both differential-tested against that meaning.

On this substrate, DN-32 (maintainer-ratified 2026-06-25) defines a **three-layer hybrid memory architecture**, with the reclamation mechanism fixed by RFC-0027 and the static-optimization leg by MEM-4/DN-33. It is the memory-tier analogue of the certification modes: the cheapest mechanism is the default, and the expensive machinery engages only when the program's structure actually demands it.

### Layer 1 — affine/linear ownership (the primary path)

Unique data is owned and *moved*, never shared. A value with a single owner carries no reference count at all; ownership transfer is a move, and reclamation is a scope-exit drop (LIFO per RT7, `Exact` by Rust's drop specification in the current runtime). This inverts the Swift/Python "everything is counted" default — Rust-style move semantics as the ambient discipline, but without a borrow checker, because with no mutation there is no aliased-mutable-state problem for a borrow checker to solve. The corpus grounding is RFC-0008's RT1 ("values move; state is never shared") and the affine `Sender`/`Receiver` channel pair (non-`Clone`, single-owner cross-hypha transfer); prior art is Clean's uniqueness types and linear type systems.

**Pro:** the common case is genuinely zero-cost, and the safety argument is structural rather than analytical. **Con:** affine ownership without borrowing means "use it twice" forces an explicit step into Layer 2, and threading unique values through code is a discipline some programmers will feel.

### Layer 2 — optimized reference counting (explicit sharing only)

When a value *is* shared: non-atomic reference counting within a hypha (the unit of concurrent execution — single-threaded inside, so no atomic traffic), Perceus/FBIP-style `rc == 1` in-place reuse so "functional but in-place" updates are recoverable, and — for the R1 release horizon — cross-hypha transfer is **sole-move only** (`RcCell` stays `!Send`; shared-across-atomic-RC is explicitly deferred to R2 as a named open question, not fudged). The atomic cost is paid only after a value genuinely crosses into concurrent use.

On top sits MEM-4/DN-33's **static RC elision** in `mycelium-mir-passes`: the Core IR is lowered to a *separate* RC-annotated IR (`RcNode` with explicit `Dup`/`Drop` operations), the pass first emits the naive fully-owned baseline, then elides operations where uniqueness or borrowing is statically provable — Increment 1 borrow-elides fully-borrowable `let` bindings, replacing `Dup`s with non-consuming `Borrow`s and a single `DropAfter`. Soundness follows the ratified differential-plus-structural-invariant strategy: a balance invariant on the emitted IR, plus a reference RC-evaluator confirming that elided and non-elided emissions reclaim the *same* value set with no use-after-free. The tagging is characteristically honest: semantics-preservation is `Empirical`, the `Dup`-count reduction is `Exact` (read off the IR), and the *performance* benefit stays `Declared` until measured on a corpus. Critically, the pass lives outside the trusted base, so a bug there is a missed optimization, never a use-after-free — the runtime count is always the sound fallback.

**Pro:** the Koka/Lean-4 lineage's performance story (non-atomic counts, reuse analysis, static elision) with a soundness architecture in which the optimizer cannot hurt you. **Con:** RC has cascade-drop latency (RFC-0027's OQ-3 pause-budget question is acknowledged and mitigated, not eliminated); `rc == 1` reuse-writes (Increment 2) and the full functional-in-place guarantee (Increment 3), interprocedural borrowing, and recursion RC are all still deferred; and sole-move cross-hypha transfer is a real expressiveness restriction today.

### Layer 3 — region-based batched scope reclamation

A scope is a region; values dying within it are reclaimed in bulk at scope exit. Parent–child reclamation is total-ordered (child before parent), but *sibling* regions reclaim **concurrently** — the resolution of RFC-0027's hardest open question (OQ-1), decided in favor of weak coupling. The argument is structural: RT7's structured concurrency already makes sibling scopes concurrent by construction, and acyclicity guarantees no cross-sibling aliases exist to police, so concurrent teardown is safe rather than merely fast.

**Pro:** bulk reclamation amortizes RC bookkeeping for scope-local allocation patterns, and concurrent sibling teardown composes directly with the structured-concurrency runtime (an orphan hypha is not grammatically expressible — every hypha lives inside a `colony` scope that cannot exit before its children resolve). **Con:** region-inference quality determines whether values are held to scope exit longer than a precise counter would keep them — the classic regions trade of peak memory for throughput.

### Never-silent reclamation

The distinctive move: every reclamation event emits a structured `ReclamationRecord` — `{scope_id, sweep_epoch, trigger, value_meta_hash, channel_id?}` — routed through a `ReclamationSink`, and a reclamation that produces no record is *defined* as a transparency violation. Three triggers are live and wired in `mycelium-std-runtime`: `RcZero` (last reference dropped), `ScopeExit` (a region closes, batch-reclaiming deferred values), and `ChannelClose` (a channel torn down with values in transit), with an end-to-end composition test exercising all three through one audit trail.

**Pro:** memory behavior becomes observable the way values are — memory management with structured logging built into the semantics, which neither Rust nor any GC'd language offers natively, and which an SRE sensibility will recognize immediately. **Con:** overhead on every reclamation path that sink implementations must keep cheap, and the audit stream is only as valuable as the tooling that consumes it.

### The defensive layer

The same philosophy shows up at the implementation level. RFC-0041 (**Enacted** 2026-07-05 via the promoted W0–W7 wave) closed the recursion-depth failure class: `Node` and `Datum` have *manual, iterative* `Clone`/`PartialEq`/`Drop` implementations — a derived recursive `Drop` would `SIGABRT` on a deeply-nested spine, a silent failure violating G2 — bit-identical to the derived forms and mutation-witnessed; the content hasher is likewise iterative; deep evaluation runs on an explicit work stack under a unified fuel budget, closing the `myc run` SIGABRT. And `Repr` deserialization's `MAX_DIM = 2^30` cap closes the crafted-descriptor over-allocation vector before any payload is materialized.

### Current frontier (from the document tail)

Two recent Accepted-not-Enacted decisions complete the design story while honestly remaining `Declared`/unbuilt on the build side.

**DN-120 — identity coherence, solved-by-design.** ADR-003's disclosed residual ("content-addressed identity ⇒ efficient temporary-copy mutation may remain to be solved") is formally ruled closed: DN-35 §5's mechanism *is* the answer — in-place reuse fires only at `rc == 1`, meaning no live alias of the old identity exists, and the intern/hash-cons table is modeled as a weak map, evicted-or-copied on reuse. `rc == 1` detection is landed `Exact`; the reuse-write itself stays `Declared`.

**DN-125 — value-threading as the native answer to `&mut`.** Mycelium's solution to the problem Rust's `&mut self`/`&mut T` solves (in-place mutation through a reference) is **value-threading**: take the receiver by value, return the mutated value, rewrite the call site to rebind (`x.f()` → `x = f(x)`). This is correct-with-a-copy immediately and zero-copy once DN-33's static uniqueness analysis and the DN-35 reuse-write land — the mechanism is answered-by-design out of already-ratified pieces, and only the transpiler *application* was genuinely open (now scoped, build issue M-1081). Two adversarial narrowings are held never-silently: unprovable uniqueness/aliasing routes to a borrow-check precondition or a uniqueness proof on the emitted Mycelium, never a possibly-divergent rebind; and interior-`&mut`-return shapes (`get_mut`, `iter_mut`, `IndexMut`) route to the Approximation/Interop-Bridge taxonomy cells, never a fabricated value-threaded form.

The upshot: Layers 1–2 are not just a reclamation strategy — they are the mechanism that makes mutation-*shaped* code expressible in a value-semantic language without a reference type ever entering the model. Today that is honest-copy performance; the zero-copy half rests on the two still-deferred DN-33 increments, and the design says so.

## Positioning: What Sets Mycelium Apart


**Against Rust.** The same safety *outcomes* — no use-after-free, no data races, no leaks in safe code ("in safe Mycelium a memory leak is not expressible") — reached by removing mutation instead of proving borrow discipline. The cost is giving up in-place mutation as a programming model and betting the performance gap on Perceus-style reuse plus regions plus the AOT path; the recent DN-125 value-threading design shows how mutation-*shaped* code stays expressible without a reference type ever entering the model. What Mycelium has that Rust does not: representation and trustworthiness in the type system, content-addressed identity, and audit records for reclamation.

**Against the Perceus family (Koka, Lean 4, Roc).** The same reclamation lineage — precise RC over acyclic immutable values, reuse analysis, static elision — but layered *under* affine-first ownership (so the unique path pays no RC at all) and *over* concurrent region reclamation, with a never-silent audit-record requirement none of them have, and with the elision pass architecturally outside the trusted base so optimizer bugs cannot become memory unsafety.

**Against Unison.** Content addressing, but the hash includes representation descriptors and static contracts — two definitions differing only in paradigm hash differently — and identity feeds a certification and deployment system (spores, hot-inject, an OCI-backed registry) rather than primarily a codebase manager.

**Against ML/array languages and VSA/HDC libraries.** Representation and numeric trustworthiness live *in the type system*, with a proven-where-provable, bounded-where-not conversion regime — instead of in tribal knowledge about which cast loses what. The VSA story in particular is unusually rigorous: six models under one trait with a normative per-operation guarantee matrix, capacity bounds that cite their theorems, resonator factorization honestly capped at `Empirical`, and reconstruction manifests that refuse to let indexed retrieval masquerade as compositional reconstruction.

**Against verification-oriented languages.** Verification is tunable rather than totalizing: the `fast`/`balanced`/`certified` modes and the guarantee lattice let a program be exactly as audited as its owner wants, with the never-silent mode tag ensuring the choice is always disclosed. The split regime matches proof burden to what is actually provable per conversion class instead of demanding uniform proof everywhere.

**The honest overall con.** This is a research-scale bet where several load-bearing legs — atomic cross-hypha sharing, reuse increments 2–3, full AOT language coverage, the self-hosted kernel, the two-mode typing axis, value-threading's zero-copy half — are, by the project's own scrupulous tagging, still `Declared` or in-flight. But the fact that the project *tells you that itself*, per claim, with the checked basis named, is arguably its most distinctive feature of all.

## Reading the Corpus: The Append-Only Supersession Strategy


### How supersession works

The strategy is append-only with explicit status promotion, never edit-in-place: documents move `Draft → Proposed → Accepted → Enacted` (house rule #3, "stepped, not skipped"), decision bodies are never rewritten, and corrections arrive as dated changelog rows or follow-on documents. The consequence is that the *tail* of the DN/ADR/RFC sequences carries the freshest truth — the latest documents refine, resolve, or supersede specific clauses of earlier ones by reference, while the earlier documents stand as the permanent record of what was decided and why.

Crucially, **Accepted ≠ built.** An Accepted DN or RFC ratifies a *design* whose every mechanism claim stays `Declared` until an implementation lands and is differential-witnessed, at which point the status row appends "Enacted — with code" naming the exact test suites and their state. The recent DN cluster makes this explicit in each status field ("Accepted, not Enacted — it builds nothing yet; every mechanism stays `Declared`/unbuilt until the FLAGGED build issue lands"). Ratification itself is a tagged claim: recent notes record the ratification basis verbatim, including when ratification happened under explicit maintainer delegation, so assent is auditable at the strength actually checked (VR-5 applied to governance itself).

### The resolution chain

`docs/Doc-Index.md` is the authoritative per-document status resolver. `docs/CURRENT-STATE.md` (refreshed 2026-07-16 at promote-to-staging close-out) is the fast pointer index — a dense, link-heavy "what's true now" — with the explicit rule that if it looks stale, the top of `CHANGELOG.md` wins. So the practical resolution path for any question is: `CHANGELOG.md` top → `CURRENT-STATE.md` → `Doc-Index.md` → the highest-numbered DN/ADR/RFC touching the question. There is exactly one formal document-level supersession so far (ADR-021 → ADR-022, the release gate), with the retired document archived verbatim to a protected branch rather than deleted — the same archive-never-lose discipline ADR-043 applies to retired Rust code.

### Versioning as an honesty artifact

The workspace version itself follows the tagging discipline: `0.463.1` was cut on 2026-07-16 as the first *history-derived* SemVer — computed `Empirical` from the Conventional-Commit stack on the dev branch (each `feat` a minor bump under major-version-zero, each `fix`/`perf` a patch) rather than declared, with the note that main's squash-only history alone would only justify `0.19.0` and that dev granularity is the chosen basis until the tree decomposes into component repos.

### Current gate and freeze state

As of the analyzed checkout: the ADR-022 release-gate tracks stand at T2–T7 **done** (surface language, runtime, stdlib on the ADR-035-narrowed bar, FFI, AOT, toolchain), T1 (kernel) **gate-met but tag-reserved** to the maintainer (M-703), T8 (docs/stability) blocked only on the maintainer's own release act (M-738), and T9 (self-hosting) explicitly **not tag-gating** (ADR-036/038's function-first release strategy).

The most important structural fact for reading the tail: the kernel/L0/L1/lexicon **freeze** declared 2026-07-02 (DN-56, on the DN-76 green scorecard, with post-freeze kernel diffs restricted to the DN-39 class) was **suspended by ADR-045** (Accepted 2026-07-10, maintainer-ratified) for a bounded gap-closure window, so the language can close its expressibility gaps early rather than after the stdlib fans out into per-component repos. The ADR-042 end-state (full `.myc` self-hosting, kernel included) is unchanged; re-freeze triggers only once the DN-99 residual worklist (M-1024…M-1034) is exhausted, a successor scorecard re-scores green, and a follow-up maintainer-ratified ADR reinstates the diff policy. The DN-119–140 cluster documented in the tails writeup is that window's output.

## The Implemented Grammar


The normative oracle is `docs/spec/grammar/mycelium.ebnf` (450 lines, W3C-EBNF notation per RFC-0006's deliberate rejection of ISO 14977) paired with a 57-program accept/reject conformance corpus — every accept program must parse by the grammar, every reject program must not, and the reject set includes cases like reserved-word-in-identifier-position so the "lexes as keyword, opens no production" discipline is itself tested. The implemented lexer/parser lives in `crates/mycelium-l1` (`token.rs`, `lexer.rs`, `parse.rs`), and the `Tok::` enum matches the EBNF's vocabulary one-for-one, including the reserved-not-active words held as keywords.

### Program structure

`program ::= phylum_header? nodule_block+`. A `phylum` header groups multiple nodules in one source file as pure metadata (identity stays per-nodule per ADR-003), with `pub` exports and `use a.b.X` / `use a.b.*` glob imports under deterministic shadowing — a local declaration beats an explicit `use`, which beats a glob, and a glob-versus-glob collision on a *referenced* name is a never-silent ambiguity refusal. The `;` is a **mandatory component terminator** (DN-57/M-818) — uniformly, even after `}`-closed blocks and after the nodule header itself, which makes fully whitespace-free source legal (`nodule d; fn a() => … = …;`) and gives a streaming parser the guarantee that a completed component is emittable the instant `;` arrives; the terminator adds no AST node. Top-level items are unordered for name resolution: the elaborator builds the call graph over the nodule and auto-groups each strongly-connected component of two or more functions into an inspectable `FixGroup` node (a self-looping singleton becomes `Fix`; a non-recursive function inlines) — mutual recursion is inferred, never keyworded. A nodule header may carry the atomic `@std-sys` attribute, the audited FFI-floor marker that is the only context where a `wild` block is legal.

### Items

The item forms are `use`, `default paradigm` (the RFC-0012 ambient — at most one per nodule, paradigm only, never inserting a swap, elaborating to the same L0 as the longhand twin), `type` (ADT sums of constructors with `[T]` type parameters and `{N}` const/width parameters — RFC-0037's bracket-family deconfliction, which retired `<…>` type arguments entirely), `trait` (a named set of `fn` signatures), `impl` in both trait-instance (`impl Trait[args] for T { … }`, under global coherence plus the phylum-wide orphan rule) and inherent (`impl T { … }`, desugaring to top-level `fn`s) forms — disambiguated after the head by the `for`-versus-`{` follower, any other follower a never-silent parse error — and `fn` with optional `pub`, optional `thaw` (per-definition de-maturation inside a matured scope; RFC-0017 moved maturation itself to scope headers and manifests, so a bare `matured fn` is a parse error with a teaching diagnostic), an optional effect annotation `!{…}` (kernel effect kinds `retry|alloc|io|cascade|time` plus user-declared names; absence means pure, and the coverage rule is a checker obligation), and the `@tier(compiled|interpreted)` execution hint (non-semantic by NFR-7 — never a behavioral switch).

Two composition/metaprogramming items round it out. `object` groups one constructor with trait impls, `via N : Trait` field-index delegation clauses, and inherent `fn`s — pure frontend sugar desugaring to `type` + `impl` + generated forwarding impls + `fn`, zero kernel growth, `reveal`-able. And the DN-54 pair `lower Name[params] = expr` / `derive Name for T` — user-extensible generative lowering, with surface and structural checks (rule-name uniqueness, `derive` name resolution) landed and the RHS elaboration to L0 honestly flagged as a never-silent `Residual`.

### Types

The type grammar covers the four paradigm families plus their RFC-0037 D2-b short aliases (`bin`/`tern`/`emb`/`hvec`, canonicalized to the long form by `mycfmt`; `vec` was considered and rejected for colliding with `std.collections.Vec`), `Substrate{tag}` for affine external resources, `Seq{elem, len}` and `Bytes` (RFC-0032), nullary `Float` (ADR-040, IEEE-754 binary64 only at introduction, later widths append-only), tuple/product types of arity ≥ 2 desugaring to synthetic single-constructor data types with no new L0 node, paradigm-less shapes like `{8}` resolved by the enclosing ambient (an ill-fitting shape is an explicit `ParadigmShapeMismatch`; no ambient in scope is an `UnresolvedAmbient`), named types with `[…]` type arguments, and first-class function types `A => B` — right-associative, with `->` retired and met by a never-silent teaching-reject diagnostic rather than silently aliased. The signature move: `@ Exact|Proven|Empirical|Declared` guarantee annotation on a type, binding tighter than `=>` (so `A @ Exact => B` parses as `(A @ Exact) => B`), making the honesty lattice a type-level index in the concrete syntax (LR-6).

### Expressions

The expression forms: `let … in`; `if/then/else`; `match` over one scrutinee with or-patterns (`A | B => e`, alternatives required to bind the same names at the same types) and wildcard/literal/tuple/constructor patterns; `for x in xs, acc = init => body` as elaboration-defined sugar for a structurally-recursive fold, total by construction — `while`, `loop`, `break`, `continue`, and `return` are deliberately *not* forms and *not* reserved, receiving teaching diagnostics instead; `swap(e, to: T, policy: p)` with target and policy always lexical; `with paradigm P { … }` block-scope ambient overrides that fill interior tags and elaborate away — never a conversion, with unbridged cross-paradigm edges a static `MissingConversion`; `wild { … }` as the denied-by-default unsafe escape (grammatically legal anywhere so the corpus can parse it, checker-gated to `@std-sys` nodules with the `!{ffi}` effect declared, body audited-not-verified, execution staged); `spore(e)` building a reconstruction manifest; `consume e` acquiring exclusive ownership of an affine `Substrate` (type discipline checked, runtime affinity honestly a `Residual`); the structured-concurrency fragment `colony { hypha e, … }` where an orphan `hypha` is grammatically inexpressible, reference semantics is spawn-order sequentialization (RT2), the colony yields the last hypha's value, and a `hypha` may carry a `@forage(bitmask)` D-lite placement policy (semantics-free per RT3 — placement changes performance, never the observable, with an all-zero mask an explicit `NoCandidates` refusal); `fuse(a, b)` lawful semilattice merges over a type with a declared `Fuse` instance, result metadata the meet of the inputs; `reclaim(policy) { … }` attaching a reified supervision/reclamation policy to a structured scope; and `lambda(x, y) => e` closures that curry (a multi-parameter lambda desugars to nested single-parameter closures, so partial application works) and lower via Reynolds defunctionalization — no closure heap object, no new kernel node, and a zero-parameter lambda a never-silent refusal.

### Operators and literals

The symbolic operator layer (RFC-0025) is frontend-only sugar: each operator desugars to a canonical word-function call (`a + b` → `add(a, b)`, `-a` → `neg(a)`), so there is no L0/L1 kernel change and the word form stays valid everywhere. Precedence follows Rust's table; every binary operator is left-associative; prefix operators bind tighter than every binary. The angle-bracket operators are fully wired because RFC-0037's move of type arguments from `<…>` to `[…]` removed the collision: `<<`/`>>` sit at shift tier, `<`/`>` at comparison tier, and the two-character `<=`/`>=` glyphs are *retired* (the worst post-type-argument ambiguity) — their word forms `lte`/`gte` have no glyph and parse as ordinary calls. Application and ascription: `app_expr ::= primary ('(' args? ')')* (':' type_ref)?`, ascription binding loosest.

Literals: `0b` binary with underscores; `0t` balanced trits, most-significant-first over `+ 0 -`; `0x` bytes with an even hex-digit count enforced; string literals with a deliberately minimal escape set (`\n \t \\ \" \0 \r`, no raw newlines, and deliberately no `\xNN` because it would let a "textual" literal inject a non-UTF-8 byte) lowering to the same `Repr::Bytes` value form as hex literals, UTF-8 encoded; float literals denoting the correctly-rounded (round-to-nearest-even) binary64 value of their decimal text, with structural int-disambiguation (`1.` stays `Int` plus the path glyph; no leading-dot form) and never-silent refusals for empty exponents and literals whose rounded value is not finite (`1e999` is a lex error — a literal is a conversion boundary, so out-of-range never silently lands on ±inf); and `[e1, …, en]` list literals constructing `Seq{T, N}` — or, per RFC-0040, desugaring to a right-nested `Cons(e1, Cons(…, Nil))` chain when checked against a *cons-list-shaped* user ADT, recognized structurally rather than by name, coexisting with `Seq` without ambiguity.

### The lexicon: active versus reserved

Active fungal-themed constructs: `nodule` (module), `phylum` (library-scale grouping), `spore` (reconstruction manifest / deployable unit), `substrate` (affine external resource), `colony`/`hypha` (structured concurrency), `fuse` (semilattice merge), `reclaim` (supervised scope), `forage` (placement policy, D-lite subset), `matured`/`thaw` (maturation and its inverse), `wild` (the unsafe escape), and `germinate` (spore deployment, in tooling). Reserved-not-active — lexed as keywords so they can never silently become identifiers, but with no production consuming them: `mesh` (decentralized gossip/pub-sub overlay), `graft` (capability contract with infrastructure), `cyst` (durable checkpoint / dormant resumable form), `xloc` (explicit value trans-location), and `backbone` (priority transport path) — the RFC-0008 R2 runtime vocabulary, pre-reserved so activating each later is an append-only event. `grow` is held as reserved-but-superseded-by-`derive`. The implemented token set also carries `wrapping` (the RFC-0034 §10.1 Axis-B opt-out block, landed in `mycelium-l1` pending ratification) and the full scalar/strength/sparsity keyword vocabulary.

## The Document Tails: Latest Decisions and What They Change


Because the corpus is append-only with explicit supersession (see the documentation-strategy document), the highest-numbered RFCs, ADRs, and DNs carry the freshest design truth. This document inventories the tails as of the analyzed checkout.

### Late RFCs (0036–0041)

**RFC-0036 — Kernel Primitives Consolidation** (Accepted 2026-06-28) ratified the single-frozen-L0-kernel position: nine of ten nodes judged irreducibly primitive, with `FixGroup`'s derivability the one flagged open question — to be examined before freeze, a derivability question rather than an Accept blocker.

**RFC-0037 — Surface Grammar Deconfliction and Layout Independence** (**Enacted** 2026-06-27, full corpus migrated, `mycelium-l1` 615 + `mycelium-fmt` 11 tests green) is the collision-free bracket-family allocation woven through the grammar document: `[…]` type arguments, `{…}` const/width parameters, `=>` as the return arrow with `->` retired, the `0t` trit-literal prefix, the `lambda` keyword, and the short paradigm-type keywords.

**RFC-0038 — Inject-Mode Security Axis** (Accepted 2026-06-29) adds a security axis to the hot-inject path; the design is ratified but explicitly not built — every mechanism claim stays `Declared` until the Implementation DoD lands.

**RFC-0039 — Native Dense and VSA Codegen** (Accepted 2026-06-30, the four §8 open questions resolved same day) decides how `Repr::Dense` element-wise operations plus the ADR-030 quantization descriptor lower natively — with quant granularity, accumulator width, and packing schedule recorded as inspectable `Meta.physical` — how `Repr::Vsa` bind/bundle/permute lower across the BSC/HRR/FHRR/MAP-I/MAP-B/SBC models honoring ADR-031's element-space/sparsity/complex-carrier commitments, how the dynamic-VSA JIT runs (data-dependent dimension, runtime model selection, cleanup/resonator loops) as an explicitly-selected `ExecMode`, and the verification contract: every native Dense/VSA operation differential-checked against the reference interpreter.

**RFC-0040 — Vec/List Literal Desugaring** (Accepted 2026-07-03, implemented and green — 1854 tests) resolved the list-literal question: `[e1, …, en]` checked against a *cons-list-shaped* user ADT desugars to the right-nested `Cons(e1, Cons(…, Nil))` chain, with a structural recognizer for "cons-list-shaped" rather than a name match, composing unambiguously with the existing `Seq{T,N}` literal.

**RFC-0041 — Recursion-Depth Safety** (**Enacted** 2026-07-05 via the promoted W0–W7 wave, PR #1155) closed the deep-recursion `SIGABRT` class: explicit work stacks, a unified fuel budget, and manual iterative `Clone`/`PartialEq`/`Drop`/hashing on the recursive types — resolving DN-84.

### Late ADRs (040–045)

**ADR-040** (scalar float value form, Route II) and **ADR-041** (MSRV → 1.96.1) are woven through the code: the binary64-only `Float` repr, canonical-NaN identity, string wire forms for IEEE specials. **ADR-042/043** (Accepted 2026-07-07) are the self-hosting commitment pair: freeze the Rust base, rewrite everything to Mycelium under the "zero foreign first-party languages" end-state, retire each Rust component only on a dual witness (Rust differential plus native `myc` toolchain), archive never delete. **ADR-044** admitted `tokio`+`axum` to the workspace *solely* for the tero HTTP front, scoped to one crate, plain HTTP on a token-scoped `127.0.0.1` floor with TLS explicitly a reverse-proxy concern — and a subsequent trim pass extracted `mycelium-tero` verbatim into its own published repo (`tzervas/tero-rs`), removing the async runtime from the workspace entirely. **ADR-045** (Accepted 2026-07-10) is the currently-governing document: the whole-project unfreeze for a bounded gap-closure window — suspending the DN-56 kernel freeze, the L0 Core IR floor, the L1 primitive/type set, the L2/L3 surface grammar freeze, and the stdlib lexicon freeze — with the ADR-042 end-state unchanged and re-freeze bound to exhausting the DN-99 residual worklist plus a re-scored green scorecard plus a follow-up ratified ADR.

### The DN gap-closure cluster (119–140)

This cluster — the output of the ADR-045 window — is a coherent program: for every Rust construct the `mycelium-transpile` crate gaps on, a DN establishes Mycelium's *native answer* under the DN-110/111 translation taxonomy (Native Equivalent / Idiomatic Remapping / Approximation / Interop Bridge), preserving value semantics and KC-3's zero-kernel-growth bar, with verify-first grounding against exact source lines.

**DN-119** set the frame: the target is reframed from "L3 grammar accepts every Rust construct verbatim" to "**L3 expresses the native answer to every construct's underlying problem**" — the honest form of full native capability — with a verified inventory separating genuine L3 grammar gaps from work misattributed to L3 that actually belongs to the kernel, runtime, or transpiler.

**DN-120** formally closed ADR-003's disclosed residual — content-addressed identity versus efficient temporary-copy mutation — as **solved-by-design**: DN-35 §5's mechanism (reuse fires only at `rc == 1`, i.e., no live alias of the old identity; the intern/hash-cons table modeled as a weak map, evicted-or-copied on reuse) *is* the coherence answer. Detection landed `Exact`; the reuse-write stays `Declared`.

**DN-123** corrected the record/named-fields framing: the surface is already substantially supported — the transpiler carries a field-name↔index map (`StructLayout`) and desugars struct literals, projection, and struct-update to positional data.

**DN-125** decided **value-threading** as the native answer to Rust's `&mut self`/`&mut T`: take the receiver by value, return the mutated value, rebind at the call site (`x.f()` → `x = f(x)`) — zero-copy via the already-ratified DN-33 static uniqueness plus DN-35 `rc == 1` reuse plus DN-120 identity coherence, correct-with-a-copy until those land. Two adversarial narrowings are held never-silently: unprovable aliasing routes to a borrow-check precondition or a uniqueness proof, never a possibly-divergent rebind; interior-`&mut`-return shapes (`get_mut`, `iter_mut`, `IndexMut`) route to Approximation/Interop-Bridge, never a fabricated value-threaded form. The mechanism was answered-by-design; only the transpiler application was genuinely open, now scoped as M-1081.

**DN-126** ratified **two-mode typing** as a genuinely third orthogonal axis — type-resolution *strictness*, distinct from certification depth (`fast`/`certified`) and from guarantee grade (the lattice). Loose mode is the existing bidirectional checker in a non-refusing posture over the unchanged repr-dynamic evaluator; strict mode is the same checker with demotion off, gating compilation unconditionally; mechanical strictification writes down only *principal* inferred types, with non-principal/undecidable holes definitionally surfaced through the DN-04 diagnostics channel, never guessed. The runnable floor (name/arity/parse/FFI refusals) stays hard even in loose mode. Zero kernel growth, and the explicit enabler for Python transpilation (PEP-484 gradual typing maps onto loose mode directly).

**DN-127 through DN-138** work the same pattern across the stdlib-trait surface: `Display`/`format!` becomes a pure value-returning render `T → Bytes` with interpolation as `bytes_concat`, dissolving the `&mut Formatter` accumulator entirely (DN-127); `#[derive(…)]` becomes a standard-derive *lowering library* built on the already-landed DN-54 `lower` facility (DN-128); `Default` becomes an `Init` prelude trait seeded like `Fuse`/`Ord3`, alongside an `Error` prelude (DN-129); parametric instance heads `impl[T] Trait for Foo[T]` monomorphize dictionary-free as a family of concrete instances with the coherence rules extended (DN-130); bounds on inherent-impl type parameters discharge by redistribution onto the lifted methods, reusing the landed fn-bound path (DN-131); of the three pattern-surface forms only struct-variant patterns are a genuine transpiler gap, with range and `@`-binding patterns routed to desugarings rather than new productions (DN-132); qualified associated calls `Type::method(…)` emit through the deterministic `{Type}__{method}` mangle already minted on the declaration side (DN-133); expression-position enum struct-variant construction closes the `struct_layout`-population gap (DN-134); the Result/Option combinator surface is verified *already native* in `lib/std` — the real gap is closure-argument lowering (DN-135); the emit dispatch gets an interfaces-first refactor of its three residual sub-dispatch axes (DN-136); `Unit` becomes a nullary-constructor ADT, the arity-0 member of the tuple family, no new kernel node and no grammar change (DN-137); and primitive/std-type derive instances become available in freshly-transpiled files via prelude-instance seeding so the landed derive rows compose `myc check`-clean code (DN-138).

**DN-140** landed as code on the analysis date (M-1106): one total, idempotent, position-independent function `valid_ident` mapping arbitrary strings to legal Mycelium identifiers — subsuming the reserved-word escape (`_kw`), the nodule-path escape, and the illegal-character class (`_u{HEX}_`) — plus an injective length-prefix mangler, with every non-identity rewrite emitting a `// Declared: renamed …` comment.

### What the tail changes about the data and memory model story

Three of these are direct amendments to the core analysis, and they are the current design frontier.

**The mutation story is now formally closed at the design level** (DN-120 + DN-125). The memory model's Layers 1–2 gain their ergonomics layer: they are not just a reclamation strategy but the mechanism that makes mutation-*shaped* code expressible in a value-semantic language without a reference type ever entering the model. The pro is that the whole `&mut` problem dissolves into machinery already committed to; the con is that the zero-copy half rests on the two still-deferred DN-33 increments, so today it is honest-copy performance — and the design says so.

**A third orthogonal axis enters the model** (DN-126). Alongside certification depth and guarantee grade, type-resolution strictness gives a fast duck-typed dev loop and a source-language-general porting path (Python first) without a new type theory. The pro is iteration speed with the compiled path's rigor untouched; the con is that it is Accepted/`Declared` (M-1077 unbuilt), and a third axis is a third thing every consumer of a value's trust story must understand.

**The physical leg of the data model is getting real** (RFC-0039 plus the codegen tail). Native Dense/VSA lowering with quantization granularity, accumulator width, and packing schedules recorded as inspectable `Meta.physical` — plus the dynamic-VSA JIT as a named `ExecMode` — means "packing is a schedule, not a type" now has an AOT enforcement story, every native operation differential-checked against the reference interpreter per the standing discipline.

The meta-observation stands even stronger at the tail than at the head: the newest documents are the most methodologically rigorous — verify-first grounding against exact source lines, self-corrections of their own task framings recorded verbatim ("no sycophancy" is a literal house rule), ratification-under-delegation with the basis recorded, and the Accepted/Enacted seam never blurred. For anyone, human or agent, trying to determine what is true now, the resolution path is: `CHANGELOG.md` top → `CURRENT-STATE.md` → `Doc-Index.md` → the highest-numbered document touching the question — which is exactly how the strategy was designed to work.
