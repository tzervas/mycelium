## §1 The question (restating DN-110 §8.2)

DN-110 §5-A generalizes DN-54's landed term-level lowering (`lower`/`derive`, applied at a **type/item**
position — J1) to **expression position** (J3: the `format!`/`matches!`/`swap!` role): a sugar rule applied at a
use site, whose invocation arguments are substituted into the rule's RHS term, the whole lowering to L0 as a
hand-written expression would. §8.2 flags three open sub-questions this raises that the type-position model does
**not** answer:

- **(i) Resolution.** Do free identifiers in the RHS resolve at the rule's **definition site** (true hygiene) or
  the **use site** (Rust's `$crate` / hygiene-escape need)?
- **(ii) Capture-avoidance.** When the RHS introduces its own binders, how are use-site argument terms
  substituted **capture-avoidingly**?
- **(iii) Affine interaction.** Does a sugar introducing an affine `Substrate` binder (DN-71) or a `consume`
  interact soundly with the use-site's affine accounting?

DN-110's recommended stance was "definition-site resolution and capture-avoiding substitution as the default."
The maintainer's directive is to **dig harder and experiment** — pressure-test that stance against the actual
codebase and design the experiments that would validate or break it, rather than adopt it on paper.

## §2 Why DN-54's type-position hygiene does NOT extend (be specific)

DN-54 §4.3 declares user lowering rules "hygienic by construction … variable capture is **structurally
impossible** for a typed-AST lowering rule" (DN-54 lines 175–193). That claim is **true for the type/item
position it was made about, and its proof is a *non-interleaving* argument** — which is exactly what expression
position breaks:

- In **item position** (`derive Name for T`), DN-54 §10 ratified **Model A — sibling-item injection** (DN-81 /
  M-973; DN-54 lines 557–567): the rule's output is a whole `impl` block injected as a **peer of hand-written
  impls**, flowing through the normal coherence/dispatch path. An `impl` block **opens its own fresh scope**; it
  is never spliced *into* a surrounding expression that carries use-site **local** bindings. The def-site RHS
  scope and any use-site local scope **never meet** — so there is no interleaving in which a name could capture.
  "Hygiene by construction" there is really "no two scopes ever interleave."
- In **expression position**, interleaving is the *whole point*. The argument terms are **use-site expressions
  that carry use-site free variables** (locals, `let`-bindings, `lambda` params); they are substituted **into** an
  RHS that carries the rule's **own** binders; and the result is planted **at the use site**, inside the use-site
  local scope. Two scopes now meet at the splice — the def-site body and the use-site arguments — and that meeting
  is precisely where capture lives (the classic `swap!(a, t) → let t = a; …` capturing a use-site `t`).

So DN-54's structural guarantee is not *wrong*; it is *scoped* — it is a corollary of item-injection's
non-interleaving, and it **silently lapses** the moment the same mechanism is asked to splice a term into an
expression context. §8.2 is a genuinely new surface for exactly this reason. (This confirms DN-110 §8.2's "NEW
model, not inherited" framing, and pins *why* to Model A's non-interleaving.)

## §3 Ground truth from the codebase (Empirical — this is what changes the analysis)

Four facts read directly from `crates/mycelium-l1` and `mycelium-interp` reshape the design. They are the reason
this note reaches a *more tractable* verdict than §8.2's "sharpest open question" framing — evidence-led, not to
please (VR-5 applied to agreement, house rule #4).

1. **L0/L1 binders are named strings, not de Bruijn indices.** `Expr::Let { name: String, … }`
   (`ast.rs:714`), `Expr::Lambda { params: Vec<Param>, … }` (`ast.rs:871`), `Expr::For { x: String, acc:
   String, … }` (`ast.rs:743`); the interpreter core mirrors this — `Node::Lam { param, body }`, `Node::Let {
   id, … }`, `Node::Var(String)` (`mycelium-interp/src/lib.rs`). **Consequence:** capture is *representable*
   (two same-named binders can shadow), and — see §6 — **content-addressing does not canonicalize
   alpha-equivalence for free.**

2. **The runtime substitution is capture-UNSAFE but sound — because it only ever substitutes *closed* values.**
   `subst` (`mycelium-interp/src/lib.rs:819`) handles **shadowing** (a re-binding of `var` blocks substitution
   in the body — the `Let`/`Match`-arm cases) but performs **no alpha-renaming** of a bound variable to dodge a
   free variable in the substituted term. β-reduction `App{Lam{x,e},a} ⟶ e[x↦a]` (`lib.rs:75`, `lib.rs:580`) is
   sound only under the invariant that "an elaborated `Lam` is **closed** (no captured environment)"
   (`lib.rs:64`) — i.e. the substituted `a` is a **closed value**, so it has no free variable a body binder
   could capture. **This soundness argument does not transfer to compile-time sugar expansion**, where the
   substituted argument is an **open use-site term** carrying free use-site variables. The one place Mycelium
   substitutes today is safe *for a reason that expression-position sugar deletes.*

3. **The elaborator already has a working half-hygiene mechanism: the `%`-namespace gensym.** `Elab::fresh(base)`
   (`elab.rs:1093–1097`) mints binder names containing `%`, and its doc states: *"`%` is not an identifier
   character in the surface lexer, so fresh names can never capture or collide with surface binders."*
   `elaborate_lower_rule` already uses it (`elab.rs:601–604`: the synthetic RHS entry is `%lower-rhs%<name>`),
   and the `?`/`for` desugars use it for their introduced binders. This is a landed, `Empirical`, **Barendregt-by-
   partition** trick: elaborator-introduced names live in a namespace **provably disjoint** from every surface
   identifier. It is precisely the capture-avoidance primitive §8.2(ii) needs — already built, just not yet
   applied to *user sugar-rule* RHS binders.

4. **`elaborate_lower_rule` today only elaborates *nullary, closed* expression RHSs.** It builds a synthetic
   zero-parameter entry and elaborates its body (`elab.rs:605–627`); an entry with value parameters is a
   never-silent `Residual` (`elab.rs:641–645`). So the **argument matcher plus parameter substitution is exactly
   the genuinely-new machinery** — everything downstream of it (RHS type-check `check_lower_rule_rhs_type`,
   acyclicity `check_lower_rule_acyclicity`, KC-3 guard, and the **affine tracker M-919 that already runs on a
   `lower`-rule RHS**, `affine.rs:25`) is landed and reused.

## §4 The hygiene model (Declared — the proposal)

The model is a **three-color partition** made cheap by finding #3, plus a resolution rule and an affine rule.
Because Mycelium substitutes into an **already-typed term whose binder namespace is partitionable**, the
scope-set bookkeeping that a token-rewriting macro system needs (Racket) collapses to a namespace discipline.

**(A) RHS binders → freshened into the `%`-namespace (capture-avoidance, sub-question ii).** Every binder a sugar
RHS introduces (`let`, `lambda` param, `for`, `match`-arm binder, a `Substrate`/`consume` binder) is renamed
through `Elab::fresh` at expansion into a **per-expansion-site** `%`-scope, e.g. `%sugar#<rule>@<siteN>%tmp`.
Since `%` is unreachable from surface (finding #3), **no rule binder can ever be the same name as any use-site
variable**. Capture of a use-site free variable by a rule binder is therefore **impossible by construction** —
*not* by a runtime search, but by namespace disjointness. This reuses landed machinery; it is the same guarantee
DN-54 §4.3 claimed, re-established for the interleaving case by the partition rather than by non-interleaving.

**(B) Use-site argument terms → substituted verbatim, capture-safe by (A).** The argument terms carry only
**surface-namespace** free variables. The RHS binders after (A) are **`%`-namespace** names. The two namespaces
are disjoint, so a straight structural substitution of arguments into the RHS **cannot** capture: there is no
`%`-binder that could shadow a surface free variable, and no surface free variable that could be captured by a
`%`-binder. **No on-the-fly alpha-renaming at splice time is required** — the freshening in (A) already
discharged capture. (This is the elegant simplification the `%`-gensym plus value-semantics substrate buys over a
general capture-avoiding `subst`.)

**(C) RHS free identifiers → resolved at the rule's DEFINITION scope (resolution, sub-question i).** Recommended
default: **def-site resolution**. A free identifier in the RHS (a helper `fn`, a constructor, a `std` path) is
resolved **at rule-definition time** to a fully-qualified, content-addressed L0 reference (ADR-003), so what is
spliced is a **resolved reference**, not a bare name that a use-site local of the same spelling could re-bind. A
use site with a local `helper` therefore cannot hijack the RHS's intended def-site `helper`. Any *use-site*-
resolution need (the `$crate`/hygiene-escape case) is an **explicit, flagged opt-in parameter** of the rule, never
the default — matching the `syntax-rules`/Lean consensus (research/25 Topic 1). This is the one part with real
residual risk under separate compilation / cross-phylum use (§10, the expression analogue of DN-54 §10 OQ-D).

**(D) Affine/type soundness → checked on the EXPANDED L0, never laundered by the sugar (sub-question iii).** The
sugar does not get to *assert* soundness; the **expanded** L0 term is run through the already-landed checks: the
§4.1 IL-grammar RHS type-check, the KC-3 kernel-growth guard, and the **M-919 affine tracker** (`affine.rs:25`,
which already tracks a `lower`-rule RHS). Consequences: a rule that binds a `%`-fresh `Substrate` binder is
tracked **independently** of any use-site affine binder (distinct names ⇒ no double-count); a rule that uses its
parameter **more than once** duplicates whatever it is bound to, so when bound to a **use-site affine `Substrate`
value** the expansion is a never-silent affine violation caught on the expanded L0 (`Expr::Consume` requires a
`Substrate` operand and refuses otherwise, `ast.rs:816` plus `checkty`); a rule that uses its parameter **zero
times** drops a linear argument — likewise caught. **The sugar can express a non-linear pattern, but it cannot
*hide* one** — the transparency spine (§5) plus post-expansion affine checking make the violation visible.

**Naming:** this is the `syntax-rules`/Lean-macro def-site-hygiene consensus (research/25 Topic 1;
Ullrich–de Moura typed-AST hygiene), *specialized* to a language that (a) substitutes typed terms not tokens and
(b) already owns a disjoint gensym namespace — so the "engineer the hygiene algorithm" cost that sinks §5-B
(pattern macros) is **already paid** for the dominant hazard.

## §5 Preserving `reveal` / M-1051 inspectability (house rule #2, DN-106, DN-38 §7)

Hygiene must not make the expansion un-inspectable. Under this model, `reveal` shows the **actual expanded L0
term** — the `%`-freshened binders and the def-site-resolved references are **real, named nodes in the frozen
core**, exactly the term the kernel runs (DN-38 §5). There is no lossy text reconstruction (the §5-B weakness).
Two fidelity points, one of which is a genuine tension flagged for experiment E3:

- **`%`-names are not surface-re-parseable.** A revealed L0 term containing `%sugar#…%tmp` **cannot be
  re-lexed as surface** (`%` is forbidden in the surface lexer — the very property that makes it hygienic). So a
  naïve "print surface, re-parse" round-trip **fails**. The round-trip discipline (DN-38 §7 / research/25 Topic 5,
  Lean's injective-`pp`) must therefore be defined **at the L0-term level** (`delaborate∘lower = id` re-elaborates
  the *shown L0 term*, not re-parses printed surface) — **or** `reveal` must pretty-print `%`-binders back to
  **display-only, surface-legal, guaranteed-fresh** names (`tmp#1`) with a documented alpha-renaming, accepting
  that the display form is a view, not the identity witness. This is a real sub-decision (§10 OQ-H3), and E3 is
  designed to force it into the open rather than discover it after M-1051 ships.

- **The guarantee-tag ratchet still binds (DN-110 §8.5, VR-5).** The expanded L0 must propagate the **weakest**
  guarantee tag of the substituted arguments and the RHS — a sugar may never let its expansion claim a stronger
  tag than its parts. This is a checker obligation on the expanded term, co-designed with (D).

## §6 The "content-addressed L0 gives hygiene for free" hypothesis — CORRECTED

DN-110's framing (and the task prompt) floated that content-addressed frozen L0 terms might give hygiene partly
for free ("no free identifiers to capture at L0"). **Read against the code, this is only half-true, and the
false half matters:**

- **False half:** because binders are **named strings** (finding #1), two alpha-equivalent terms (`λx.x` vs
  `λy.y`) have **different structure and therefore different content hashes** unless hashing is performed over an
  alpha-canonical (de Bruijn / normalized) form. Nothing in the read path shows such a pre-hash normalization.
  So content-addressing **does not** collapse alpha-equivalence, and capture **is** representable. Hygiene is
  **not** free from content-addressing alone. Claiming otherwise would be an un-grounded upgrade (VR-5).
- **True half (and it is the load-bearing one):** what *does* give the dominant hazard "nearly for free" is
  **finding #3 — the `%`-namespace partition** — together with **value semantics** (no shared mutable state ⇒ no
  "action at a distance" capture, DN-110 §5-A). Content-addressing then contributes the *right* thing: once (C)
  resolves RHS free names to content-addressed references, **same-intent expansions dedup by hash** (ADR-003,
  DN-54 §4.4) and def-site references are stable identifiers, not spellings. So the honest statement is: *hygiene
  is cheap here not because L0 is content-addressed, but because the elaborator already partitions the binder
  namespace; content-addressing makes def-site resolution and dedup clean on top of that.*

This correction is the single most decision-relevant result of the deep-dive: it moves the capture-avoidance
sub-question (ii) from "needs a new alpha-renaming subsystem" (the §8.3 KISS/YAGNI worry that could tip J3 to the
Rank-2 fallback) to "reuse a landed one-line gensym discipline."

## §7 Experiment plan (the core deliverable — design only; DO NOT implement here)

Prototypable in `crates/mycelium-l1` (semcore lane — these are **specifications for an implementer**, not code
to land in this note). Harness shape throughout: an **in-crate white-box test module** (`crates/mycelium-l1/src/
tests/hygiene_expr_sugar.rs`, per the repo's in-crate `src/tests/` rule), **table-driven** over a corpus of
triples `(sugar-definition, use-site, oracle)`, where the **oracle** is the hand-written capture-free L0 the
expansion must match (the DN-38 §7 differential). Assert with structural **alpha-equality** (a helper the harness
must define, since finding #1 means `==` is not alpha-aware) and with **observational equality** (run both through
the `mycelium-interp` evaluator on a generated input corpus — value equality of results, clean under value
semantics, research/25 Topic 5). Prototyping order is E1, then E3, then E2, then E5, then E4 (dominant hazard
first, then the round-trip tension, then resolution, then affine, then nesting).

### E1 — Capture-avoidance (the `swap!` classic) — validates (A)+(B), sub-question (ii)

- **Setup.** Define an expression sugar whose RHS introduces a binder that **collides by spelling** with a
  use-site variable: `sugar swap2(a, b) = let t = a in (b, t)` invoked at a site where `t` is a live use-site
  local, e.g. `let t = 7 in swap2(1, t)`.
- **Property (capture-avoidance).** With (A) freshening the RHS `t` to `%…%t`, the use-site `t` in the argument
  `b = t` must still observe **7**; result `(7, 1)`. The un-hygienic expansion (reuse literal `t`) would capture,
  yielding `(1, 1)`.
- **Assert.** `alpha_eq(expand(site), oracle)` **and** `observe(expand(site)) == observe(oracle)` over the input
  corpus, where `oracle` is the hand-freshened L0.
- **PASS:** freshened-binder expansion equals the oracle for **every** collision case in the table (including
  arguments that mention the RHS's raw binder spelling). **FAIL:** any case where the use-site variable's value
  is altered by expansion — that is a live capture bug and is disqualifying (house rule #2).

### E2 — Def-site resolution / referential transparency — validates (C), sub-question (i)

- **Setup.** A sugar RHS references a **def-site** helper: in nodule `A`, `fn helper(x) = x + 100` and `sugar
  bump(v) = helper(v)`. Invoke it in nodule `B` that defines a **different** local `helper(x) = x - 100` in scope
  at the use site. **Include a cross-phylum case** (rule defined in phylum P, used in Q — the OQ-H1 stressor).
- **Property (referential transparency).** The RHS's `helper` must resolve to **A's** `helper` (def-site),
  independent of B's shadowing local. `bump(5)` observes **105**, never **-95**.
- **Assert.** `observe(expand) == observe(def-site-resolved oracle)`; additionally assert the expanded L0's
  reference is the **content-addressed hash of A::helper** (ADR-003), not a bare `Var("helper")`.
- **PASS:** every case resolves to the def-site binding; the flagged **use-site-escape opt-in** (when the rule
  explicitly requests use-site resolution) resolves to B's `helper` and *only* then. **FAIL:** any default-mode
  case that binds to the use-site `helper` — that is a referential-transparency break.

### E3 — `reveal` round-trip fidelity — validates §5, the flagged tension (designed to possibly BREAK the model)

- **Setup.** Expand any binder-introducing sugar from E1/E4; call the (M-1051 v0) `reveal` path on the result.
- **Property (round-trip).** Define round-trip at the **L0-term level**: `reelaborate(reveal_l0(expand))` is
  `alpha_eq` to `expand`. Separately test the **surface-print** path: assert whether `reveal`'s surface rendering
  re-lexes at all (it must **not**, for raw `%`-names) and, if a display-renaming is applied, that the renamed
  form (i) re-parses and (ii) is `alpha_eq` to the original.
- **Assert.** L0-level round-trip holds (identity up to alpha); surface round-trip **either** is explicitly
  declared out-of-contract for `%`-names **or** succeeds only through a documented display-renaming.
- **PASS:** L0-level `delaborate∘lower = id` up to alpha for the whole corpus, and the surface-fidelity decision
  (OQ-H3) is forced to one of the two documented options. **FAIL:** L0-level round-trip is **not** identity
  (reveal is lossy) — that would violate DN-106/house-rule #2 and is a **blocker signal** for Rank-1 on J3 (the
  honest disconfirming outcome this experiment exists to catch).

### E4 — Nested / recursive expansion — validates per-site scope freshness plus DN-54 §4.2 acyclicity

- **Setup.** A sugar whose RHS invokes **another** binder-introducing sugar (and a self-reference case, to
  exercise `check_lower_rule_acyclicity`): `sugar inner(x) = let t = x in t` and `sugar outer(y) = let t =
  inner(y) in (t, inner(y))`.
- **Property (per-expansion freshness).** Each expansion layer must freshen into a **distinct** `%`-scope
  (`%…@site1%t` vs `%…@site2%t`), so the two `inner` expansions inside `outer` do **not** collide and the outer
  `t` is distinct from both inner `t`s. Requires the fresh counter to be **per-expansion-site**, not reused.
- **Assert.** `alpha_eq(expand(outer(k)), oracle)` where the oracle hand-distinguishes all three binders;
  termination of expansion (acyclicity check fires on a genuinely cyclic rule, never on this DAG).
- **PASS:** all binders distinct, expansion terminates, cyclic rule is a never-silent refusal. **FAIL:** any
  cross-layer collision (a shared/global counter reused across nesting) or a non-terminating expansion.

### E5 — Capture across a value-semantic / affine boundary — validates (D), sub-question (iii)

- **Setup.** Three sub-cases with a `Substrate` argument (DN-71): (a) a **linear** sugar `use1(s) = consume s`
  invoked with a use-site `Substrate` value; (b) a **duplicating** sugar `dup(s) = (consume s, consume s)`; (c) a
  **dropping** sugar `drop0(s) = 0` (parameter unused).
- **Property (affine soundness on expanded L0).** After expansion, the **M-919 affine tracker** (`affine.rs`)
  runs on the expanded L0. Case (a) checks clean (single use); cases (b) and (c) are **never-silent affine
  violations** (double-use / drop of a linear value) — the sugar must **not** launder them.
- **Assert.** For each case, `check(expand)` matches the expected verdict (accept / refuse) exactly; the refusal
  is the structured DN-71 diagnostic, not a silent accept; a `%`-fresh RHS `Substrate` binder is tracked
  independently of any identically-spelled use-site `Substrate` binder (no double-count / no false positive).
- **PASS:** the affine verdict on the expanded L0 equals the hand-oracle verdict for all sub-cases. **FAIL:** any
  case where the sugar hides a linearity violation (b/c accepted) or manufactures a false one (independent
  binders double-counted) — either is disqualifying.

**Cross-experiment metrics (the honest scorecard).** capture-avoidance (E1, boolean per case); referential
transparency (E2, observable equality); reveal round-trip fidelity (E3, alpha-identity at L0 plus the forced
surface decision); per-site scope freshness and termination (E4); affine soundness on expanded L0 (E5). All at
LOW proptest cases per commit, HIGH on release (DN-20); each expansion is **differential and property-tested**
against a hand-written oracle (research/25 Topic 5), and — where `certified` mode is in play — **translation-
validated per run** with an emitted witness (Necula/Pnueli lineage). This is DN-38 §7's hygiene obligation made
concrete for expression position.

## §8 Soundness argument (Declared) and what still breaks it

**The argument (`Declared`).** Capture-avoidance reduces to **namespace disjointness**: rule binders live in
the `%`-namespace (A), argument free variables live in the surface namespace, the two are provably disjoint
(finding #3, `Empirical`), so substitution (B) cannot capture — no renaming search, no scope-set algebra. Referential
transparency reduces to **early binding**: RHS free names are resolved to content-addressed references at def
time (C), so use-site spelling cannot rebind them. Affine/type soundness is **not asserted by the sugar**; it is
**re-checked on the expanded L0** by the landed passes (D), so a sugar can express but not hide a violation.
Transparency is **by construction**: the only output channel is an L0 term, `reveal`-shown as the real term
(§5). Kernel cost is **zero new L0 node** (KC-3, DN-54 §6, `Proven`-by-construction over the closed `Node`
enum); the new elaborator cost is the argument matcher plus the per-site freshening loop — **bounded, and reusing
`Elab::fresh`**, not a new subsystem (this is the §8.3 KISS/YAGNI worry substantially **retired**).

**What still breaks it (residual, honest — VR-5).**

1. **Def-site resolution under separate compilation / cross-phylum use (C).** Resolving RHS free names to
   content-addressed refs at def time is clean *within* a nodule; across phyla it is the expression analogue of
   DN-54 §10 **OQ-D** (cross-phylum `derive` coherence), unsettled there and **not** settled here. If a rule is
   defined in phylum P and used in Q, *which* `helper` (and its content hash) is captured, and how does re-export
   / version skew interact? **Bounded, but real — E2 must include a cross-phylum case.**
2. **`reveal` surface round-trip for `%`-names (§5, E3).** The identity witness must be defined at the L0-term
   level, or a display-renaming pass must be built. If M-1051's v0 only offers lossy text, the *facility*'s
   transparency claim is `Declared`, not built (DN-110 §8.4) — this is a **sequencing dependency**, not a design
   hole, but it is on the critical path.
3. **Multi-output sugars (item plus expression).** DN-54 §10 OQ-C (a rule generating *both* an expression result
   and sibling items) crosses the two hygiene regimes (Model A non-interleaving *and* expression interleaving) in
   one rule. Out of scope for v0 J3; flagged so it is not a silent assumption.
4. **Guarantee-tag propagation edge cases (§5, DN-110 §8.5).** Computing the weakest tag across substituted
   arguments *and* the RHS, especially when an argument is itself a prior expansion, needs the checker obligation
   spelled out — designed-in via (D), not yet prototyped.

## E1 Result (2026-07-10) — append-only addendum, NOT a rewrite of §1–§12

This section records the **outcome** of the §7 E1 experiment this note commissioned. It adds new,
dated material only; the §1–§12 design record above is unchanged (house rule #3 — append-only).
This note's own field-table **Status** row above is deliberately left as-is: this is a companion
analysis note recording an experiment result, not a maintainer ratification of a DN status move.

**E1 (capture-avoidance) — PASSED.** `crates/mycelium-l1/src/tests/hygiene_expr_sugar.rs`
(PR 1413, merged to `dev` 2026-07-10, tracked as **M-1055**) prototypes exactly the §4 (A)+(B) model —
`%`-namespace freshening of RHS binders plus capture-safe verbatim substitution — against 4 core
collision fixtures (`binder_shadows_use_site`, `arg_mentions_raw_binder_spelling`,
`multi_param_used_twice`, `nested_binder_in_rhs`), each checked by **two independent oracles**:
structural `alpha_eq`-to-a-hand-built-oracle, and observational `mycelium_interp::Interpreter::eval`

**Non-vacuity, grounded (the mutation-test finding).** The harness's own PR records a live negative
control: freshening was temporarily disabled in `expand()` (RHS binders kept their literal spelling,

independently (an `alpha_eq` mismatch, *and* a wrong evaluated value, e.g. `2` instead of the
expected `8`), before the change was reverted. This is stronger than a single-oracle mutation check:
it shows neither oracle alone is satisfiable by a vacuous/broken implementation — `alpha_eq` failing
and the *independent* `Interpreter::eval` witness failing **on the same disabled-freshening mutation**
confirms the corpus is non-vacuous by construction, not merely by one check that could itself be
miscalibrated. (Framed against the specific worry this addendum was asked to address — "stubbing
`alpha_eq` to always pass plus disabling freshening" — no such stub was literally run as a separate
experiment; the grounded evidence is the equivalent and, read carefully, stronger claim above: the
eval witness is demonstrated to independently catch the same bug `alpha_eq` catches, so a hypothetical
`alpha_eq`-only stub could not have passed silently either.) This moves capture-avoidance for
**(A)+(B) only**, over the E1 corpus, from `Declared` to `Empirical` (VR-5 — no broader upgrade).

## E3 Result (2026-07-10, following the E1 Result addendum) — append-only addendum, NOT a rewrite of §1–§12 or the E1 Result section

This section records the **outcome** of the §7 E3 experiment, re-assigned by the E1 Result addendum
above to depend on **M-1051 Increment-3** (the `certified`-mode round-trip check, DN-38 §5). It adds
new, dated material only; the design record above (including the E1 Result section) is unchanged
(house rule #3 — append-only).

**M-1051 Increment-3 — BUILT.** `crates/mycelium-l1/src/reveal.rs` adds
[`certified_roundtrip`], the `certified`-mode round-trip check primitive Increment-1's module doc
flagged as a later increment. It always computes the **L0-term-level identity witness**
(`L0Witness`: `reelaborate` + `alpha_eq`) and, secondarily, attempts a genuine surface round-trip
(`render_surface` → real reparse → real re-check → real re-elaborate → `alpha_eq`) whenever
`render_surface` flags the text `reparseable`, reporting one of `SurfaceOutcome`'s explicit variants
rather than ever assuming success from the flag alone.

**STEP-0 FLAG-2 verify-first finding (empirical, before any test was written).** A scratch probe
(wrap `render_surface`'s output in a throwaway `fn`, run it through the real lexer/parser/checker/
elaborator) established, against the crate as it stands:

1. **`reparseable = true` is necessary but not sufficient for real re-parse.** A `Node::Lam`
   renders as `lambda(param) => body` — no `%`, no `#`, so mechanically flagged `reparseable`
   — but `crate::parse::Parser::parse_params_opt` *requires* a type annotation
   (`name ':' type`), which `render_surface` never emits. The probe reproduced this directly: the
   flag says reparseable, the real parser refuses with `expected ':' and the parameter type`.
2. **Every `Node::Op` is unconditionally non-reparseable, independent of `%`-freshening.**
   `crate::checkty::prim_kernel_name`'s surface→kernel mapping is total and every kernel prim name
   is `.`/`:`-namespaced (`bin.add`, `bit.not`, `cmp.eq`, `fuse_join:binary`, …) — there is no
   bare-identifier target anywhere in the mapping. `render_surface`'s `is_bare_ident` gate therefore
   routes *every* `Op` through the `#op[…]` marker. This means an entirely **`%`-free** arithmetic
   sugar expansion is *still* out-of-contract for the surface path — the reparseable-fragment gap is
   wider than OQ-H3's original `%`-name framing, and independent of it.
3. **Every fixture in the existing `reveal.rs` test corpus (`let_swap`, `call_inlined`,
   `self_recursion_fix`, `mutual_recursion_fixgroup`, `nested_match_construct`) is already
   non-reparseable** (each mixes `Op`/`Fix`/`FixGroup`/`Construct`/`Swap`) — confirming the fragment
   that genuinely closes end-to-end is narrow: `Const` (finite scalar payloads), `Var`, and `Let`
   built **only** from those, and **no further** (an "no-op identity-shuffle" fragment). A trivial
   `Const` program and a `let x = <const> in x` program **did** genuinely round-trip in the probe.
   **`App` and `Match` do NOT close, even when `%`-free and marker-free** — adversarially checked
   (PR #1423 review, 2026-07-11) and now test-backed
   (`crates/mycelium-l1/src/tests/reveal_roundtrip_e3.rs`'s
   `app_is_honestly_reparse_failed`/`match_is_honestly_alpha_mismatch`): `let f = 5 in f(3)` renders
   `reparseable = true` but re-check refuses (`unknown function/constructor/prim "f"` — nothing
   besides `Lam`/`Fix`/`FixGroup` is callable in the surface grammar, and those are already excluded
   above) — `SurfaceOutcome::ReparseFailed`; `let x = 5 in match x { 5 => x, _ => x }` reparses and
   re-elaborates, but the elaborator injects an extra scrutinee-binding `let`
   (`let scrut%N = … in match scrut%N { … }`) absent from the original, so the result is **not**
   `alpha_eq` to it — `SurfaceOutcome::AlphaMismatch`. Nothing containing `Op`/`Lam`/`Fix`/
   `FixGroup`/`Construct`/`Swap`/`App`/`Match` closes the surface round-trip.

Per the task's own go/no-go instruction, this finding was **not** forced past what it supports: the
surface path ships exactly as narrow as STEP-0 found it, honestly labelled at every non-closing case,
rather than papering over the gap.

**E3 — BUILT AND RUN. PASS, narrowly scoped.**
`crates/mycelium-l1/src/tests/reveal_roundtrip_e3.rs` (M-1055) composes `certified_roundtrip` over
the E1 non-vacuity recipe — a fresh, self-contained `expand` prototype (independently re-derived,
not a cross-module import of E1's), an **independently hand-built oracle** (disjoint binder
spellings from `expand`'s `%`-gensym), an `mycelium_interp::Interpreter::eval` differential that
never touches `alpha_eq`, and a mutation self-check. Four tests, all passing
(`cargo test -p mycelium-l1 --lib reveal_roundtrip_e3`):

- **`percent_free_plain_term_surface_roundtrip_closes`** — a `%`-free, `Op`-free plain term
  (`let x = 5 in x`) genuinely closes the *surface* round-trip (`SurfaceOutcome::Ok`), confirming
  the narrow fragment identified by STEP-0 is non-empty and mechanically checked, not merely
  asserted.
- **`percent_free_op_based_term_is_honestly_out_of_contract`** — an entirely `%`-free arithmetic
  term (`add(2, 3)`) is honestly `SurfaceOutcome::OutOfContract` (the `#op[bin.add]` marker),
  demonstrating STEP-0 finding 2 directly: the Op gap is independent of `%`-freshening.
- **`e3_hygiene_fixtures_l0_witness_and_eval_differential`** — two hygiene-interesting fixtures
  (`swap2_classic`, `pair_add`; the `swap2` shape reused from E1, re-derived here) each check: the
  surface path is honestly out-of-contract (both a `%`-name and the `#op[…]` marker are present);
  the **L0-term witness holds** (`L0Witness::Closed`) **and** is `alpha_eq` to the **independently
  spelled oracle** — the non-vacuous check, since a `true`-always/`false`-always `alpha_eq` could
  not pass a genuinely different spelling; an `eval` differential (independent of `alpha_eq`)
  confirms the same result observationally; a captured (unhygienic) variant evaluates to a
  genuinely different, hand-derived value, demonstrating discriminating power.
- **`e3_mutation_self_check_disabling_freshening_is_caught`** — the harness-level non-vacuity
  demonstration this go/no-go explicitly asked for: `expand(..., freshen = false)` (a real,
  mechanically-produced unhygienic expansion, not a separately hand-authored literal) is asserted to
  **fail** both the `alpha_eq`-to-oracle check and the `eval`-matches-hygienic-value check. If a real
  implementation ever silently disabled `%`-freshening, this is the shape of check that would catch
  it — this test proves the shape actually works, on this harness, today.

**OQ-H3 disposition — CONFIRMED option (1)/(a); option (2) DEFERRED.** The identity witness for the
round-trip property is the **L0-term level** (`alpha_eq` over `reelaborate`'s output), never a
surface re-parse for `%`-names — `certified_roundtrip` implements exactly this (the L0 witness is
*always* computed; the surface path is secondary and `%`-free-only). A display-renaming pass
(option (2), making `%`-names themselves surface-reparseable) is **not built** and not scheduled by
this increment.

**Honest scope of the upgrade (VR-5 — narrow, not blanket).** This PASS moves **one specific claim**
`Declared → Empirical`: the L0-term-level round-trip property,
`alpha_eq(reelaborate(expand(rhs, params, args)), independent_oracle)`, over an actual `%`-freshened
sugar expansion, **scoped to the reparseable fragment** STEP-0 identified. It does **not** upgrade:
the surface `delaborate ∘ lower = id` obligation for `%`-names (stays out-of-contract/unbuilt,
by design); any claim about `Op`/`Lam`/`Fix`/`FixGroup`/`Construct`/`Swap` surface round-tripping
(all stay out-of-contract, empirically confirmed non-reparseable or non-parseable); and it does
**not**, by itself, upgrade this note's §8.4 reveal-transparency claim to more than "the L0-term
witness is checked, over a narrow fragment, for one hygiene mechanism (expression-position `%`-
freshening)" — the **facility itself (M-1054)** and **full reveal-transparency** (a source-span
`site` resolver, a `reveal` CLI, `certified`-mode wiring into the checker — M-1051 Increment-2 and
beyond) remain unbuilt. **DN-110 stays `Accepted`, NOT `Enacted`.**

**Net effect on this note's own E1+E3 go/no-go for J3.** With E1 PASSED (prior addendum) and E3 now
also BUILT AND RUN (PASS, narrowly scoped, this addendum), M-1055's Definition of Done — "E1+E3
built and run" — is **satisfied**. This is **not** the same claim as "the hygiene model is fully
validated" (E2/E5 remain unbuilt, §9's tractability verdict stays partially evidenced exactly as the
