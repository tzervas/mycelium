## 1. Why this ADR exists

CHANGELOG says *"Semantic versioning will begin when the kernel stabilizes"* but **no release
gate defines "stabilizes"** (remediation roadmap §1, lines 16–17). ADR-018 sets the per-crate
SemVer *policy* (0.y.z pre-1.0; no crate to 1.0.0 without an explicit decision; source-only
distribution) but deliberately leaves the *readiness criteria* to a separate decision. This ADR
is that decision's substrate.

Per the remediation roadmap's framing (§1, lines 26–30): **the road to 1.0.0 is not "more
features" — it is closing the honesty-integrity gap and making the guarantees durable, then
ratifying an explicit release gate.** Phases 0–3, 5, 7, 8 are already complete in design + build;
the project is *capability-ready*. What remains is ratification, not construction.

## 2. Scope — 1.0.0 is the kernel/core

Recommended framing (remediation roadmap §5): ship a **1.0.0 of the kernel/core** — the honest,
verified value-semantics substrate (Core IR, reference interpreter, certified binary↔ternary
swap, VSA/dense ops with honest bounds, the shared certificate checker, selection + EXPLAIN, the
trusted toolchain) — and **scope the concrete surface language to a tracked `1.x`**. This lets the
verified substrate (the actual product) reach 1.0.0 without being held hostage to surface-language
ratification.

## 5. Out of scope for 1.0.0 (Phase-3+ maturation, not release blockers)

Confirmed from the roadmap (remediation §5): native libMLIR/LLVM codegen, JIT, semantic
projections (FR-C1), resonator factorization (FR-C2), BitNet packed-ternary acceleration (FR-C3),
the native ternary-hardware path, and **self-hosting** (Phase 5 M-502). These are maturation; the
interpreter is the trusted base and the AOT path's maturity (Phase 3) is sufficient for 1.0.0.
The concrete **surface language** is scoped to `1.x` (KC-2-gated ratification — §2, B1).

## 6. What ratifying 1.0.0 will require (the act, later)

When Gate A's open rows close and Gate B's RFC reconciliation lands, the maintainer makes the
conscientious "ship 1.0.0 of the kernel/core" decision and records it by moving this ADR
`Proposed → Accepted` (the criteria are agreed) and then, at the tagged release,
`Accepted → Enacted` (1.0.0 cut). Per ADR-018, each crate advancing to 1.0.0 is still its own
explicit decision; this gate governs the *kernel/core* release as a set.
